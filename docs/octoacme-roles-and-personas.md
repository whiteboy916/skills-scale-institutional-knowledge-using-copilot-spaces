# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **With QA Lead**: Collaborate on quality requirements and test coverage expectations
- **With Technical Architect**: Align on technical standards and design patterns
- **With Release Manager**: Ensure code is deployment-ready
- **With Project Manager**: Provide status updates and risk identification

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **With Stakeholder Representative**: Understand customer needs and feedback
- **With Project Manager**: Align on scope, timeline, and resource constraints
- **With Technical Architect**: Evaluate technical feasibility of requirements

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **With Risk Manager**: Identify, assess, and monitor project risks
- **With Release Manager**: Coordinate release schedules and dependencies
- **With Developers**: Provide scope clarity and remove blockers
- **With Product Manager**: Balance priorities and resource allocation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release schedules, manage deployment dependencies, and ensure smooth transitions from development to production. They serve as the primary coordinator for all release-related activities and communications.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release schedules and timelines
- Manage release dependencies and prerequisites
- Ensure deployment readiness and rollback procedures
- Communicate release status to stakeholders
- Maintain release documentation and post-release reports
- Manage release risks and mitigation strategies
- Coordinate with infrastructure/operations teams

### Goals
- Ensure zero or minimal downtime during releases
- Maintain clear communication throughout release cycle
- Enable rapid rollback if issues arise
- Build confidence in deployment processes

### Typical Communication
- Release kickoff meetings and planning sessions
- Deployment readiness checklists and sign-offs
- Status updates during release windows
- Post-deployment reviews and lessons learned

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Coordinate release timing and overall project schedule
- **With Developers**: Verify code readiness and deployment requirements
- **With QA Lead**: Confirm testing completion and quality sign-off
- **With Technical Architect**: Coordinate infrastructure and deployment architecture
- **With Documentation Specialist**: Ensure release notes and deployment documentation are complete

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define and maintain quality standards, develop testing strategies, and manage defect identification and resolution. They ensure that deliverables meet quality expectations and are fit for release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Develop and execute comprehensive testing strategies
- Manage defect identification, prioritization, and resolution
- Report on quality metrics and trends
- Collaborate on test case development and automation
- Conduct quality reviews and gate approvals for releases
- Mentor team members on quality best practices

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality deliverables that meet user expectations
- Reduce defects reaching production
- Build a quality-first culture across the team
- Provide data-driven quality insights

### Typical Communication
- Quality review meetings and test planning sessions
- Defect reports and quality dashboards
- Release readiness assessments
- Best practice documentation and training

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on quality requirements, test coverage, and issue resolution
- **With Release Manager**: Provide quality sign-off for release deployment
- **With Product Manager**: Align on acceptance criteria and quality expectations
- **With Technical Architect**: Ensure quality standards are feasible and scalable

---

## Stakeholder/Client Representative

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Representatives serve as the voice of customers and business stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle. They ensure that project decisions align with business needs and customer expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Represent customer and stakeholder interests and feedback
- Provide clarity on business requirements and priorities
- Participate in requirements gathering and validation
- Communicate project progress to stakeholders
- Provide sign-off and approval on major deliverables
- Escalate business-critical issues and decisions
- Gather and relay customer feedback to the team

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers maximum business value
- Maintain stakeholder confidence and engagement
- Align project decisions with business strategy
- Minimize rework due to misaligned requirements

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder meetings and demos
- Requirements clarification sessions
- Project status reports to executive leadership
- Feedback collection and dissemination

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Manager**: Communicate customer needs and business priorities
- **With Project Manager**: Provide stakeholder perspective on trade-offs and decisions
- **With Development Team**: Explain business context for requirements
- **With Technical Architect**: Translate business needs to technical feasibility

---

## Documentation Specialist

### Role Summary
Documentation Specialists ensure comprehensive, accurate, and accessible documentation of processes, deliverables, and system design. They support knowledge transfer and organizational learning.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain process documentation
- Document system architecture, design decisions, and technical specifications
- Develop user guides and training materials
- Ensure documentation aligns with delivered functionality
- Maintain documentation standards and templates
- Manage release notes and deployment documentation
- Archive and organize historical documentation

### Goals
- Enable rapid onboarding and knowledge transfer
- Reduce organizational knowledge silos
- Support regulatory compliance and audit requirements
- Improve team efficiency through clear documentation

### Typical Communication
- Documentation planning and review meetings
- Technical specification and design documentation
- Process documentation and best practice guides
- Training sessions and knowledge transfer events

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Understand technical implementation details
- **With Product Manager**: Document requirements and product decisions
- **With Project Manager**: Ensure process documentation is current and accessible
- **With Release Manager**: Create and maintain release notes and deployment guides

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects provide technical leadership, define architectural standards, and ensure solutions align with long-term technical strategy. They guide technical decision-making across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design standards
- Provide technical guidance on major decisions
- Ensure scalability, performance, and security requirements are met
- Review and approve technical designs
- Identify and mitigate technical risks
- Mentor developers on architectural best practices
- Maintain technical standards documentation

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable, and secure systems
- Reduce technical debt and complexity
- Ensure long-term technical sustainability
- Foster a culture of architectural excellence

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review boards and design sessions
- Technical design documentation and standards
- Risk assessment and mitigation planning
- Technical mentoring and knowledge sharing

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Guide technical implementation and design decisions
- **With Product Manager**: Evaluate technical feasibility of requirements
- **With Project Manager**: Identify technical risks and dependencies
- **With QA Lead**: Define testability and performance requirements
- **With Release Manager**: Ensure architectural readiness for deployment

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers specialize in identifying, assessing, and mitigating project and organizational risks. They proactively work to minimize threats to project success and organizational objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and assess project risks and opportunities
- Develop and maintain risk registers
- Create mitigation and contingency plans
- Monitor risk status and escalate emerging threats
- Facilitate risk assessment meetings and workshops
- Report on risk metrics and trends to leadership
- Build organizational risk awareness and resilience

### Goals
- Minimize project disruptions and delays
- Enable informed decision-making through risk visibility
- Build organizational risk management capability
- Protect organizational assets and reputation

### Typical Communication
- Risk assessment workshops and planning sessions
- Risk register reviews and updates
- Risk status reports to project leadership
- Mitigation planning and contingency discussions

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Coordinate risk identification and mitigation strategies
- **With Developers**: Identify and assess technical risks
- **With Technical Architect**: Evaluate architectural risks and mitigation options
- **With Release Manager**: Assess release and deployment risks
- **With Product Manager**: Identify market and business risks
